Student Development Programmes (SDP)

Welcome to the Student Development Programmes at VIT, where we believe in the holistic development of our students beyond the confines of the classroom. Here we present a comprehensive approach to nurturing the skills required to excel in the global professional environment.

Our SDP focuses on three core areas:

  • Soft Skill Development: Communication, teamwork, and leadership skills workshops.
  • Technical Skill Enhancement: Coding boot camps, hackathons, and tech talks by industry leaders.
  • Wellness and Mindfulness: Stress management, meditation sessions, and sports activities.

The objectives of our SDP are to:

  1. Equip students with essential life skills.
  2. Prepare students for the challenges of the professional world.
  3. Encourage creativity and critical thinking.
  4. Promote physical and mental well-being.

Design Thinking (DT)

Our DT curriculum is a revolutionary approach that empowers students to tackle complex problems with innovative solutions. This human-centered methodology drives creativity and innovation at VIT.

DT is structured around five stages:

  • Empathize: Understanding the needs of users.
  • Define: Framing the problem.
  • Ideate: Generating creative solutions.
  • Prototype: Creating models of potential solutions.
  • Test: Evaluating the effectiveness of solutions.

Our DT program aims to:

  1. Cultivate a mindset of innovation and problem-solving.
  2. Encourage interdisciplinary collaboration.
  3. Develop prototypes for industry and community issues.
  4. Integrate feedback to refine solutions.

Course Projects

Course Projects are a cornerstone of the academic experience at VIT, enabling students to apply their knowledge in practical, team-based projects that often span multiple semesters.

Key aspects of Course Projects include:

  • Industry Collaboration: Partnering with companies to work on current challenges.
  • Research Opportunities: Conducting original research under faculty supervision.
  • Capstone Projects: Culminating projects that synthesize course learning outcomes.

The objectives of our Course Projects are to:

  1. Develop a deep understanding of subject matter.
  2. Enhance critical thinking and problem-solving skills.
  3. Encourage teamwork and effective communication.
  4. Promote innovation and original research.
